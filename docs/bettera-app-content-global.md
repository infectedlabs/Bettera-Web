### Feature 1 — Blood Report Analysis

```
FEATURE NAME: Smart Blood Report Analysis
TAGLINE: Your blood numbers decoded into food decisions.

WHAT IT DOES:
You photograph or upload your blood test report.
Bettera's AI reads and extracts your key health markers.
Maps your values against clinical reference ranges.
Identifies which markers are abnormal and by how much.
Translates those numbers into specific dietary priorities.
Creates a personalized nutrition profile from your actual biology —
not from an age-and-gender average.

WHY THIS MATTERS:
Two people can both have Type 2 Diabetes.
One has HbA1c at 6.8 with controlled glucose.
The other has HbA1c at 9.4 with high triglycerides.
They need different meal plans.
Bettera creates different meal plans for them.
Most apps do not.

BLOOD MARKERS BETTERA READS:

Metabolic / Diabetes Markers:
- HbA1c (glycated hemoglobin)
  Reflects 3-month average blood sugar.
  Normal: below 5.7%
  Pre-diabetes: 5.7-6.4%
  Diabetes: 6.5% and above
  Bettera adjusts carbohydrate quality and quantity
  based on the actual HbA1c value — not just the diagnosis.

- Fasting Blood Glucose
  Normal: 70-99 mg/dL (3.9-5.5 mmol/L)
  Indicates baseline glucose regulation.

- Post-Prandial Glucose (2-hour PP)
  Normal: under 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L)
  Indicates how the body handles meals.

- Fasting Insulin
  Identifies insulin resistance.
  Critical for PCOS, pre-diabetes, and obesity management.

Lipid Profile:
- Total Cholesterol (optimal: under 200 mg/dL)
- LDL Cholesterol (optimal: under 100 mg/dL)
- HDL Cholesterol (optimal: above 60 mg/dL)
- Triglycerides (optimal: under 150 mg/dL)
- LDL/HDL Ratio
Bettera adjusts fat type and quantity recommendations
based on the complete lipid picture.

Thyroid Panel:
- TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
  Normal: 0.4-4.0 mIU/L
  Hypothyroidism: above 4.0
  Hyperthyroidism: below 0.4
- Free T3 and Free T4
- Anti-TPO Antibodies (Hashimoto's marker)
- Anti-TG Antibodies

Kidney Function:
- Creatinine
- BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen)
- eGFR (estimated glomerular filtration rate)
Important for diabetics — kidney impairment
changes protein and phosphorus recommendations significantly.

Liver Function:
- ALT, AST, ALP
- Bilirubin
- Albumin
Affects protein and fat recommendations.

Complete Blood Count:
- Hemoglobin (low = anemia — adds iron-rich foods to plan)
- WBC and Platelets

PCOS-Specific Panel:
- LH/FSH Ratio
- Total and Free Testosterone
- DHEA-S
- Prolactin
- AMH (Anti-Mullerian Hormone)

HOW IT WORKS IN THE APP:
Step 1: Tap "Upload Blood Report" on the home screen
Step 2: Take a photo of your printed report
        OR upload a PDF from your device
        OR enter key values manually
Step 3: AI processes the report in under 60 seconds
Step 4: Your health profile is created showing:
        - Which values fall outside normal range
        - Severity classification (mild / moderate / significant)
        - Dietary priorities based on your specific values
        - Estimated impact timeline for dietary changes
Step 5: Your personalized 7-day meal plan is generated

COMPATIBLE REPORT FORMATS:
Works with standard blood test formats from any country.
Any report that follows standard clinical formatting —
whether from a private lab, hospital, or national health service —
can be read by Bettera's AI.

USER BENEFIT:
"Your meal plan is built around your actual numbers —
not around the average person with your condition.
Because your biology is yours alone."

```
FEATURE NAME: Smart Meal Timing
TAGLINE: When you eat is as important as what you eat.

WHAT IT DOES:
Provides condition-specific meal timing guidance.
Sends reminders calibrated to the user's schedule.
Educates on chronobiology — how time of day
affects how the body processes different foods.

MEAL TIMING BY CONDITION:

Diabetes:
- Breakfast within 1-2 hours of waking
  (skipping breakfast increases glucose variability)
- Meals every 3-4 hours to maintain stable glucose
- Larger meals earlier in the day
  (insulin sensitivity is higher in the morning)
- Dinner before 8pm where possible
- Post-meal movement reminder
  (10-15 minutes of walking after meals
   reduces post-prandial glucose by up to 22%)

PCOS:
- Front-loaded eating (larger breakfast, smaller dinner)
- Regular meal timing to support cortisol regulation
- Avoid skipping meals (worsens insulin resistance)
- Intermittent fasting guidance:
  12:12 or 14:10 windows are well-tolerated
  Aggressive 16:8 protocols not recommended
  for most PCOS patients due to cortisol impact

Hypertension:
- Consistent meal times help regulate BP rhythms
- Sodium intake distributed through the day
  rather than concentrated at dinner
- Light evening meals — heavy late dinners
  associated with elevated nocturnal BP

Thyroid:
- Thyroid medication on empty stomach
  (30-60 minutes before first meal)
- Calcium and iron supplements
  spaced at least 4 hours from medication
- Coffee and high-fiber foods after medication window
- These timing recommendations integrated
  into the morning notification sequence

NOTIFICATION TYPES:
- Meal time reminders (user sets preferred meal windows)
- Hydration reminders
- Post-meal movement reminder (diabetes users)
- Medication spacing reminder (thyroid users)
- Weekly plan refresh notification (every Monday)
